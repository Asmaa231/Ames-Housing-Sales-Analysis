# 🏠 Ames Housing Sales Analysis of 2742 houses (2006-2010)  & Interactive Dashboard
### Transforming Raw Data into Strategic Real Estate Insights

## 📋 Project Overview
This project involves a comprehensive analysis of the **Ames Housing Dataset**. Using advanced Excel techniques,
I cleaned, processed, and visualized housing trends to identify key factors influencing property prices in Ames, Iowa.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Skills (Excel and Power Up)
* **Microsoft Excel LTSC 2021:** Advanced formulas and data cleaning.

* **Power Pivot &  Data Model:** Creating relational data structures.

* **Power Measures & DAX formulas:** To calculate key performance indicators (KPIs) such as
                                  Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth, Median Sales Price, and Running Totals,
                                  ensuring dynamic and accurate real-time analysis across different neighborhoods.

* **Interactive Dashboards:** Implementing **Slicers** and **Report Connections** for dynamic filtering.

* **Data Visualization:** Pivot Charts, Trend Analysis, and YoY Growth metrics.

* **📱Mobile-Optimized Design**
To ensure accessibility, this Power BI dashboard includes a **customized Mobile Layout**. 
* User-Friendly Navigation:** Optimized for vertical scrolling and touch interaction.
* On-the-go Insights:** Key KPIs (Sale Price & Growth Rate) are prioritized for mobile viewing.
* Responsive Visuals:** Charts and slicers are rearranged for clarity on smaller screens.

## 📊 Key Insights
* Identified a **Sharp Decline** in annual growth rate (approx -49.46%) in (2099-2010).
* Visualized the correlation between **Lot Area** and **Sales Price** across different neighborhoods.
* Ranked the **Top 5 Premium Neighborhoods** by total sales volume.

## 🏁 Conclusion & Business Insights

The analysis of the **Ames Housing Dataset** reveals several critical trends that directly impact real estate investment 
and property valuation:

 **Market Volatility:** The sharp decline in the annual growth rate (~49.46%) between 2009 and 2010
                     reflects the broader economic impact of the global financial crisis on the Ames housing market.
                  
 **Value Drivers:** There is a strong positive correlation between **Lot Area** and **Sale Price**, but this varies significantly by neighborhood.
                   Premium neighborhoods like **Northridge** and **Somerset** consistently command higher prices regardless of lot size,
                   indicating high demand for location-specific prestige.
                   
 **Strategic Recommendations:** Investors should focus on the "Top 5 Neighborhoods" identified in this dashboard,
                              as they show the most stable sales volume and value retention over the 5-year period.

By leveraging **Power BI** and **Advanced Excel**, this project demonstrates how raw data can be transformed into a strategic tool
